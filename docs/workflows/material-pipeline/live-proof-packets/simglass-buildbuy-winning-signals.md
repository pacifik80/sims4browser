## Strongest current positive signals

The current strongest positive signals for `Build/Buy SimGlass` are:

1. direct glass-family naming that is stronger than object-side object-glass naming
   - a future reopened packet that surfaces explicit glass-family naming without collapsing back into `GlassForObjectsTranslucent`
2. a surviving glass-family parameter packet that fits the external `SimGlass` lineage better than object-glass or generic alpha branches
3. a transparent layered-content reading that matches the current external `SimGlass` creator packet more closely than architectural object-glass workflows
4. a narrow transparent submesh or companion structure that behaves like a preserved glass-family branch rather than a whole-object object-glass material class

Safe reading:

- these are currently the strongest kinds of positive evidence
- none of them is yet proved on a reopened `Build/Buy` fixture in this workspace

## Provisional positive signals

The following are acceptable as provisional positive signals, but not decisive by themselves:

- the fixture belongs to the narrowed transparent-decor route instead of the weaker window-heavy route
- the fixture preserves a narrow transparent companion or submesh-like bundle instead of only generic transparent object identity
- the fixture remains glass-like after stronger object-side branches are checked and documented as weaker

Safe reading:

- these signals may keep a candidate alive
- they do not close a winning `SimGlass` fixture without stronger direct evidence

## Weak non-signals

The following do not count as positive `SimGlass` signals:

- aggregate survey presence such as `SimGlass = 5`
- route membership alone
- object names containing `glass`
- visual resemblance to glass
- the absence of a better branch when the only surviving explanation is generic transparency

Safe reading:

- these may justify continued search
- they do not count as `SimGlass`-positive evidence
